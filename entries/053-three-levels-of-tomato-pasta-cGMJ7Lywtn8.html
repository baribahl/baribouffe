Three Levels of Tomato Pasta

A technique note showing three increasingly involved tomato pasta approaches: improving jarred sauce, cooking down cherry tomatoes, and making spaghetti all'assassina-style pasta directly in tomato broth.

Reference

Summary

A technique note showing three increasingly involved tomato pasta approaches: improving jarred sauce, cooking down cherry tomatoes, and making spaghetti all'assassina-style pasta directly in tomato broth.

Key Points

  • Level 1: Use premade canned or jarred tomato sauce, but heat it in a pan with olive oil and basil before adding al dente pasta and pasta water.
  • Level 2: Start with olive oil and garlic, add cherry tomatoes and salt, and cook slowly until the tomatoes break down into a deep, sweet sauce.
  • Level 3: Start with garlic, chili flakes, and olive oil, then cook dry pasta directly in tomato broth in the pan.
  • The level 3 method intentionally chars and crisps parts of the pasta for texture and aroma.

Practical Use

  • Use level 1 when relying on pantry sauce but still wanting proper saucing.
  • Use level 2 for a fresh tomato sauce with minimal ingredients.
  • Use level 3 when you want a more technical, high-attention pan pasta.
