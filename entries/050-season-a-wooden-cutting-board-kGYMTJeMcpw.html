Season a Wooden Cutting Board

A maintenance technique for wooden cutting boards: wash thoroughly, dry fully, apply food-safe mineral oil generously, let it sit overnight, then wipe off the excess.

Reference

Summary

A maintenance technique for wooden cutting boards: wash thoroughly, dry fully, apply food-safe mineral oil generously, let it sit overnight, then wipe off the excess.

Key Points

  • Use butcher-block or food-safe mineral oil.
  • Wash the board with dish soap and warm water.
  • Scrub both sides and the edges.
  • Rinse after soap; the uploader clarified this in a pinned comment.
  • Dry the board completely before oiling.
  • Apply oil generously to every surface.
  • The source uses about 30-45 ml oil for the demonstrated board size.
  • Let the board sit overnight.
  • Wipe off excess oil with paper towel the next morning.

Practical Use

  • Oil more often if the board is scrubbed frequently or used heavily.
  • Do not skip the edges; they dry and crack too.
  • This is maintenance, not a substitute for cleaning after raw meat or other high-risk use.
