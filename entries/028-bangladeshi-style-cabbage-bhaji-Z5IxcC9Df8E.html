Bangladeshi-Style Cabbage Bhaji

A simple Bangladeshi-style cabbage side: onion and garlic are cooked in oil, then a full cabbage is added with salt, turmeric, coriander powder, green chili, and a little water before simmering until soft.

Needs details

Ingredients

  • Olive oil or neutral oil, quantity not specified
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • Garlic cloves, sliced, quantity not specified
  • 1 whole cabbage, sliced
  • 1 large pinch salt, plus more to taste
  • Ground turmeric, quantity not specified
  • Ground coriander, quantity not specified
  • Green chili, sliced, quantity not specified
  • A small splash of water
  • Fresh coriander, chopped

Before You Cook

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The video gives a clear method but not full quantities for oil, garlic, spices, chili, water, or coriander garnish. Treat those amounts as source-limited and adjust by taste until tested.

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan.
  2. Add the sliced onion and sliced garlic and cook until softened.
  3. Add the sliced cabbage. It will look like a lot, but it wilts down.
  4. Add salt, turmeric, and ground coriander.
  5. Stir carefully to coat the cabbage in the spices.
  6. Add the green chili.
  7. If the cabbage starts to stick, add a small splash of water.
  8. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 20 minutes.
  9. Finish with fresh coriander.

Notes

  • The source frames this as a cheap, useful cabbage dish beyond coleslaw.
  • Cook gently after adding the water so the cabbage softens without scorching.
