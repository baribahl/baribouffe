Lacto-Fermented Sparkling Drink

A homemade sparkling drink made from a sweet infusion inoculated with whey from faisselle or lacto-fermentation brine. The creator uses lactic fermentation to acidify and carbonate the drink while avoiding yeast-driven alcohol production.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

  • 750 ml water
  • 50 g sugar
  • Aromatics of choice, such as mint, dried hibiscus, or matcha
  • 200 ml whey from faisselle, or lacto-fermentation brine

Effort

3-5 days

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Sealed fermentation bottles can build pressure. Use a vessel rated for pressure with enough headspace, chill before opening, and open slowly over a sink.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The ingredient ratio comes from the pinned recipe, but the preferred bottle size and headspace are not fully specified. Keep this entry as source-limited until the vessel parameters are reviewed.

Instructions

  1. Make a sweet infusion with the water, sugar, and chosen aromatics. The video demonstrates boiled infusions with mint, dried hibiscus, and matcha.
  2. Strain the infusion if needed.
  3. Pour the infusion into a swing-top bottle or another pressure-suitable fermentation vessel.
  4. Let the liquid return to room temperature.
  5. Add about 200 ml whey or lacto-fermentation brine.
  6. Close the bottle.
  7. Keep it at a warm room temperature.
  8. Swirl or gently shake the closed bottle once per day without opening it.
  9. After 3 to 5 days, chill and open carefully. The drink should be lightly acidic and sparkling.

Notes

  • The video says this works with any sweet liquid, but the base ratio from the pinned comment is 750 ml water, 50 g sugar, and 200 ml whey or brine.
  • A commenter notes that the quantities may exceed a common 750 ml bottle; use a vessel with enough headspace and pressure tolerance.
