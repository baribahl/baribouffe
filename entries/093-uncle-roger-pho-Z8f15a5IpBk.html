Uncle Roger Pho

A beef pho workflow with toasted spices, charred onion and ginger, cleaned beef bones and meats, daikon-sweetened broth, fish sauce, rock sugar, flat rice noodles, bean sprouts, and thin beef slices cooked by the hot broth.

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: The thin beef slices are cooked by the heat of the broth. Make sure the broth is boiling-hot when poured, and fully cook the beef for vulnerable diners.

Needs details

Instructions

  1. Toast coriander seed, star anise, fennel, cardamom, and cinnamon until slightly golden and fragrant.
  2. Transfer the toasted spices to a strainer or spice bag.
  3. Char onion and ginger.
  4. Soak flat rice noodles for 30-45 minutes.
  5. Put the beef bones and meats in cold water with salt and lemon.
  6. Massage and rinse the meat to help keep the broth clear.
  7. Use hot water to clean the meat again, pushing out impurities.
  8. Add the cleaned meats and bones to the broth pot.
  9. Add the spice strainer, peeled charred onion, charred ginger, and daikon.
  10. Season with rock sugar, fish sauce, salt, and optional MSG.
  11. Simmer for about 5 hours.
  12. Remove vegetables and aromatics carefully.
  13. Taste the broth; it should be sweet, salty, meaty, and clear.
  14. Cook the soaked noodles very briefly.
  15. Blanch bean sprouts.
  16. Assemble noodles, bean sprouts, and thin beef slices in bowls.
  17. Pour boiling-hot broth over the beef to cook it.

Notes

  • Daikon is described as a family-secret addition that helps sweetness and broth clarity.
  • The recipe references Auntie Thuy's Little Viet Kitchen cookbook and meal kit.
  • The video uses several beef cuts for depth rather than only bones.
