Jean Sulpice Preserved Lemons

A chef-style preserved lemon condiment: untreated lemons are washed, blanched three times to reduce bitterness, covered with a spiced sweet-salty brine, sterilized in jars, cooled, and left for several weeks before use.

Instructions

  1. Wash the untreated lemons.
  2. Put the lemons in cold water, bring to a boil, then remove them.
  3. Change the water and repeat the blanching process two more times, for three blanchings total.
  4. Make a brine with water, sugar, coarse salt, juniper, pepper, coriander, and star anise.
  5. Pack the blanched lemons into clean jars.
  6. Bring the brine to a boil.
  7. Pour the hot brine over the lemons until covered.
  8. Close the jars.
  9. Sterilize the closed jars, either in a steam oven or submerged in boiling water, according to a tested process.
  10. Let the jars cool.
  11. Wait several weeks before tasting.

Notes

  • Jean Sulpice describes preserved lemon as a condiment he always keeps in his kitchen.
  • The lemon can be used in sweet or savory contexts.
  • A viewer comment gives an easier Moroccan-style salt-packed lemon method, but that is a different recipe and is not used as the main source here.
