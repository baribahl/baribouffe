Magnolia Blossom Ice Cream Concept

Before You Use This

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Only use edible, correctly identified magnolia flowers from untreated trees. Avoid roadside, sprayed, or otherwise contaminated flowers.

Summary

A food concept rather than a complete recipe: magnolia petals are used to flavor an ice cream base, a magnolia flower syrup, syrupy petals, and a crunchy magnolia cookie crumble inspired by Alexis Nicole Nelson.

Key Points

  • The creator uses saucer magnolia petals for their ginger-like flavor.
  • Petals are steeped with cream and milk, strained, and used as the ice cream base.
  • A separate magnolia flower syrup is made with water, sugar, and more petals.
  • Some syrupy petals are reserved and mixed into the churned ice cream.
  • Unopened flowers are frozen and grated into a cookie dough inspired by BlackForager/Alexis Nicole Nelson.
  • Cookie crumbs are coated in leftover magnolia syrup and baked into a crunchy candy-cookie crumble that can survive in frozen cream.
  • The final ice cream combines magnolia base, syrupy petals, and magnolia cookie crumble.

Practical Use

  • Treat this as inspiration until a tested formula is found.
  • Use a trusted ice cream base and a separate tested magnolia syrup or cookie recipe if attempting a version.
  • Keep crunchy inclusions dry or candy-coated so they do not turn soggy in the freezer.
