Tofu Summer Rolls with Peanut Sauce

Two large rice-paper summer rolls filled with fried tofu, rice noodles, bell pepper, cucumber, carrot, and lettuce, served with a quick garlic-lime peanut sauce.

Needs details

Ingredients

For the rolls:

  • 400 g tofu
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 2 Persian cucumbers
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 4 large iceberg or romaine lettuce leaves
  • 2 portions rice noodles, according to package serving size
  • 8 rice paper sheets
  • Oil, for frying tofu

For the peanut sauce:

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 15 g peanut butter
  • 15 ml soy sauce
  • 4-8 g sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 15 ml sesame oil
  • 30 ml water, plus more as needed

Before You Cook

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The pinned recipe gives rice noodles as "two portions," so the exact noodle weight depends on the package serving size. Use the package portion as the working measure until tested.

Instructions

  1. Slice the tofu into strips.
  2. Pat the tofu dry with paper towel.
  3. Fry the tofu over medium-high heat until golden, then set aside.
  4. Slice the bell pepper, cucumbers, and carrot into thin strips.
  5. Prepare the rice noodles according to package directions.
  6. Make the peanut sauce by mixing garlic, peanut butter, soy sauce, sugar, lime juice, sesame oil, and water until smooth. Add more water if needed.
  7. Dip each rice paper sheet in water for 5 seconds per side.
  8. Lay sheets slightly overlapping to form a larger square.
  9. Place one lettuce leaf in the center.
  10. Add rice noodles, bell pepper, cucumber, carrot, and fried tofu.
  11. Top with another lettuce leaf.
  12. Fold in the sides and roll tightly into a large burrito shape.
  13. Serve with peanut sauce.
