Hot Sauce Template

Reference Use caution

Before You Use This

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Do not assume homemade hot sauce is shelf-stable just because it contains vinegar. Verify pH below 4.0 with a reliable meter or strips and use sterilized bottles. If in doubt, refrigerate.

Summary

A scalable cooked hot sauce template: combine peppers and aromatics with vinegar and salt, cook until soft, blend, adjust, strain, bottle, and verify acidity before treating it as shelf-stable.

Key Points

  • Base ratio: 100 g trimmed peppers plus aromatics, 40 g vinegar or acid, and 5 g salt.
  • Optional components: spices, sweetener, fruit, and extra liquid for texture.
  • Peppers can include jalapeno, habanero, or similar chilies.
  • Aromatics can include garlic and onion.
  • Fruit can be used as part of the aromatic/flavor base.
  • Cook the mixture over low to medium heat until softened, about 20 minutes.
  • Blend smooth, then thin with water or more acid if needed.
  • Strain for a smoother sauce.
  • Bottle in sterilized containers.

Practical Use

  • Scale the formula by weighing trimmed peppers and aromatics together first.
  • Use vinegar or lemon juice to lower acidity.
  • Taste after blending, then adjust salt, sweetness, spice, and thickness.
  • Check pH before pantry storage; the source says shelf-stable sauce must be below pH 4.0.
  • Refrigerate if pH is not verified.
