Bacon Grease Mayonnaise

A bacon-flavored mayonnaise made by emulsifying one egg yolk with apple cider vinegar, black pepper, melted bacon grease, and neutral oil.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

  • 1 egg yolk, preferably pasteurized
  • 5 ml apple cider vinegar or other good vinegar
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Melted bacon grease, quantity not specified
  • Neutral oil, quantity not specified
  • No added salt unless needed; bacon grease is already salty

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: This is a raw egg yolk mayonnaise. Use pasteurized egg if possible, keep it refrigerated, and discard it if it smells off, separates badly, or has been left warm.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The video demonstrates the emulsion technique but does not give the total fat quantity or bacon-grease-to-neutral-oil ratio. Treat this as a source-limited method until the fat ratio is tested.

Instructions

  1. Put the egg yolk in a tall blending jar.
  2. Add vinegar and black pepper.
  3. Melt the bacon grease.
  4. Cut the bacon grease with neutral oil so the mayonnaise does not turn into a solid block in the fridge.
  5. Add the melted fat and oil to the jar.
  6. Place an immersion blender at the bottom of the jar.
  7. Start blending from the bottom, then slowly pull the blender upward as the emulsion forms.
  8. Taste and adjust pepper or salt only if needed.

Notes

  • The neutral oil is functional, not just filler: it keeps the chilled mayonnaise from setting too hard.
  • This would likely work best in potato salad, sandwiches, or as a smoky dip.
Source notes

Source Notes

  • Test and record the total fat quantity.
  • Test the minimum neutral oil needed to prevent the mayo from hardening in the fridge.
  • Add storage duration after testing with pasteurized egg.
