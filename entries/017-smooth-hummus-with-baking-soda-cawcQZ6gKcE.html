Smooth Hummus with Baking Soda

A texture note for very smooth hummus: instead of peeling each chickpea, soften the chickpeas with baking soda, cool them completely, then process with ice and the usual hummus flavorings.

Reference

Summary

A texture note for very smooth hummus: instead of peeling each chickpea, soften the chickpeas with baking soda, cool them completely, then process with ice and the usual hummus flavorings.

Key Points

  • The source explicitly pushes back on the idea that every chickpea must be peeled for smooth hummus.
  • The technique is to boil canned chickpeas with baking soda.
  • Let the chickpeas cool completely before blending.
  • Process with ice, lemon juice, tahini, garlic, and salt.
  • The ice helps the mixture process cold and smooth.

Practical Use

  • Use this as a texture technique alongside a trusted hummus ratio.
  • Start with canned chickpeas when speed matters.
  • Rinse and drain canned chickpeas before boiling them with baking soda.
  • Add lemon, tahini, garlic, and salt gradually if using a ratio from another recipe.
