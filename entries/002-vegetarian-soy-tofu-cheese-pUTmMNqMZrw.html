Vegetarian Soy Tofu Cheese

A simple firm tofu made by curdling fresh soy milk with diluted vinegar, then pressing the curds into a block. The creator frames it as a plant-based "formage" because the technique mirrors cheesemaking: curdle a protein-rich liquid, collect the curd, and give it a shape.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

  • 500 g dried soybeans
  • 6 L water, for blending the soaked soybeans
  • 120 ml vinegar
  • 120 ml water, for diluting the vinegar

Expected yield: about 6 L soy milk or about 500 g firm tofu.

Effort

about 9 hr

3 / 5 difficulty

medium-high cleanup · 4 / 5

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried soybeans in a large volume of water for 8 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked soybeans.
  3. Blend the soaked soybeans in batches with 6 L water.
  4. Line a sieve with cloth or a clean towel, then strain the blended mixture to obtain about 6 L soy milk.
  5. Bring the soy milk to a simmer and cook it for 10 minutes.
  6. Turn off the heat and let the milk cool for 5 minutes.
  7. Mix 120 ml vinegar with 120 ml water, then pour the diluted vinegar into the hot soy milk.
  8. Stir 3 to 4 times to distribute the acid, then leave the milk undisturbed for 10 minutes so it curdles.
  9. Lift out the curds and transfer them to a perforated mold or faisselle lined with cloth.
  10. Fold the cloth over the curds, place about 1.5 kg weight on top, and press for 30 minutes.
  11. Remove the tofu from the cloth. It is ready to use.

Notes

  • The source says vinegar works as the coagulant; comments discuss that other tofu coagulants may affect flavor differently.
  • A strong blender makes the soy milk step easier.
