Blanching Green Vegetables

A restaurant prep technique for keeping green vegetables bright, sweet, and easy to finish quickly: briefly cook them in boiling water, then shock them in ice water.

Reference

Summary

A restaurant prep technique for keeping green vegetables bright, sweet, and easy to finish quickly: briefly cook them in boiling water, then shock them in ice water.

Key Points

  • Blanching is a short boil followed by rapid cooling.
  • Restaurants use it to partly cook vegetables ahead of service.
  • It helps green vegetables keep a vivid color and sweet flavor.
  • It also reduces last-minute cooking pressure when serving several people.
  • The source gives broad beans about 45 seconds in boiling water before cooling.

Practical Use

  • Set up boiling water and a bowl of ice water before starting.
  • Blanch green vegetables in small batches so the water stays hot.
  • Move vegetables directly from boiling water to ice water.
  • Drain well once cold.
  • Finish later in a pan, sauce, or final dish without overcooking.
