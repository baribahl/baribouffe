Uncle Roger Egg Fried Rice

Uncle Roger's comic but usable home-style egg fried rice: garlic, shallot, egg, leftover chicken rice, soy sauce, Asian sesame oil, MSG, spring onion, chili, and wok tossing.

Instructions

  1. Use leftover chicken rice that has been refrigerated overnight so the grains separate.
  2. Smash and chop the garlic.
  3. Slice the shallot.
  4. Beat the whole egg and egg yolk together with chopsticks.
  5. Chop spring onion and chili.
  6. Heat a wok until very hot.
  7. Coat the wok with oil, then discard excess oil if seasoning the wok first.
  8. Add fresh fat for cooking.
  9. Fry garlic and shallot until fragrant.
  10. Add the egg and stir until almost fully set.
  11. Add the rice.
  12. Stir-fry and flatten clumps so the rice separates.
  13. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, MSG, and white pepper if using.
  14. Add spring onion and chili.
  15. Toss and stir until hot, fragrant, and evenly seasoned.

Notes

  • The description allows pork lard, peanut oil, or vegetable oil.
  • The pinned comment warns that the dramatic MSG pour in the video is for the camera and is too salty for actual cooking.
  • The restaurant chef's upgrade uses pork fat, white pepper, garlic chive, chili, and sambal belacan.
