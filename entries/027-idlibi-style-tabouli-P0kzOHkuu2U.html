Idlibi-Style Tabouli

A northwestern Syrian tabouli variation where fine bulgur is toasted in olive oil, seasoned with tomato paste, citric acid, and salt, then steamed and cooled before being mixed with parsley, radishes, tomatoes, green onions, lemon, and olive oil.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

For the bulgur:

  • About 90 g fine bulgur
  • 45 ml olive oil
  • About 3 g salt
  • About 2 g citric acid
  • 15 g tomato paste
  • 120 ml water

For the salad:

  • 2 bunches parsley, large stems trimmed, finely chopped
  • 4 radishes, finely diced
  • 2 tomatoes, finely diced
  • 4 green onions, finely diced
  • About 5 g salt, plus more to taste
  • 45-60 ml lemon juice, to taste
  • 45-60 ml olive oil, to taste

Effort

45-60 min including cooling

2 / 5 difficulty

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Instructions

  1. Toast the fine bulgur in olive oil until browned and blistered, with small pale specks visible.
  2. Add salt, citric acid, and tomato paste. Stir well.
  3. Add the water, turn off the heat, and cover.
  4. Let the bulgur steam for 5 minutes.
  5. Fluff the bulgur.
  6. Spread it on a flat plate and refrigerate until cool and dry.
  7. Finely chop the parsley, trying to pass the knife through it only once so it does not bruise and become wet.
  8. Dice the radishes, tomatoes, and green onions.
  9. Mix the cooled bulgur with the parsley, radishes, tomatoes, and green onions.
  10. Dress with salt, lemon juice, and olive oil to taste.
  11. Serve immediately, or make a couple of hours ahead.

Notes

  • The toasted bulgur is the defining Idlibi-style move in this source.
  • The creator says the radishes add color and crunch.
  • Add as much or as little of the cooked bulgur as you like.
