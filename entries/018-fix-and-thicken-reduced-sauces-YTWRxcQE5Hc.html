Reference

Summary

A short sauce troubleshooting note: if a reduced sauce splits because the water-to-fat balance is wrong, add water and bring it back to a boil. If a sauce is not bound enough, use a small starch slurry and cook it out.

Key Points

  • A sauce that has reduced too far can split because the balance between water and fat is off.
  • To bring a split sauce back, add a little water and reboil.
  • For extra binding, mix cornstarch with a little cold liquid off the heat so it does not form lumps.
  • Add the slurry to the sauce and cook it so the starch thickens and stabilizes the sauce.
  • Cornstarch can lighten or dull a dark brown sauce; the source compensates with red wine when keeping a dark matelote-style color.

Practical Use

  • Reach for water first when a sauce sounds or looks split after reduction.
  • Use starch as a correction tool, not as the default for every sauce.
  • If color matters, expect cornstarch to make the sauce paler and adjust the sauce base accordingly.
Source notes

Source Notes

  • No exact starch quantity is provided; use a conservative slurry and adjust.
  • The note is about sauce correction, not a standalone sauce recipe.
