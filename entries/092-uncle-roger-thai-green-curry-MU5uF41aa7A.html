Instructions

  1. Toast white peppercorns, coriander seeds, and cumin seeds until fragrant.
  2. Grind the toasted spices in a mortar.
  3. Chop the chilies.
  4. Add chilies, shallot, garlic, galangal, kaffir lime leaf, lemongrass, and coriander root to the mortar.
  5. Work into a paste, adding the toasted spice powder back in gradually.
  6. Add salt and shrimp paste near the end and work the paste until smooth.
  7. Heat coconut milk. If canned coconut milk will not split, add a little oil.
  8. Add the green curry paste and cook it into the coconut milk.
  9. Add chicken thigh.
  10. Season with fish sauce.
  11. Add Thai eggplant, Thai basil, chili, bamboo shoot, and kaffir lime leaf.
  12. Add chicken stock if the curry becomes too dry.
  13. Add palm sugar.
  14. Taste and adjust fish sauce.
  15. Add a small pinch of MSG to taste.
  16. Serve with rice.

Notes

  • The video emphasizes a mortar and pestle for the curry paste.
  • Coriander root is specifically preferred over coriander stem.
  • Chicken thigh is preferred over chicken breast.
  • Palm sugar is preferred over white sugar.
