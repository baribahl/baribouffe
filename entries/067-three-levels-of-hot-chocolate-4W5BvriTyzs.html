Three Levels of Hot Chocolate

Three hot chocolate approaches: a simple cocoa-powder version, a thick French-style melted chocolate version, and an infused spiced version with cinnamon, cardamom, and oat milk.

Reference

Summary

Three hot chocolate approaches: a simple cocoa-powder version, a thick French-style melted chocolate version, and an infused spiced version with cinnamon, cardamom, and oat milk.

Key Points

  • Level 1: heat milk, whisk in sugar, sifted cocoa powder, and a small pinch of salt.
  • Level 2: finely chop dark chocolate, melt it gently with sugar, salt, and cream, then slowly whisk in milk for a thick smooth drink.
  • Level 3: steep chocolate with brown sugar, a cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and oat milk over very low heat for about 10 minutes.
  • Salt is used in all levels to make the chocolate taste more complete.
  • The final consistency is adjusted with extra milk.

Practical Use

  • Use cocoa powder when speed and pantry ingredients matter.
  • Use chopped dark chocolate and cream when you want a dessert-like drink.
  • Use whole spices and low heat when you want aromatic flavor without adding extracts.
  • Keep the heat gentle once chocolate is added so the mixture stays smooth.
