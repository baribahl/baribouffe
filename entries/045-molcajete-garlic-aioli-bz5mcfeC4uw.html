Molcajete Garlic Aioli

A true garlic-and-oil aioli made by crushing garlic with salt in a molcajete or mortar, adding lemon juice, then slowly emulsifying with oil.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

  • 8 garlic cloves
  • Salt, quantity not specified
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Oil, quantity not specified

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: If storing leftovers, treat this as a fresh garlic-in-oil preparation: refrigerate and use promptly.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The source demonstrates the method but does not provide the oil quantity. Add oil gradually and keep this entry source-limited until a tested garlic-to-oil ratio is established.

Instructions

  1. Smash the garlic cloves to loosen the skins, then peel them.
  2. Put the garlic in a molcajete or mortar.
  3. Crush the garlic with salt until it becomes a paste.
  4. Add the lemon juice and mix well.
  5. Add oil slowly while mixing continuously.
  6. Continue adding oil and mixing until emulsified.

Notes

  • The source distinguishes true aioli from garlic mayonnaise: this version is garlic emulsified with oil rather than an egg-based mayonnaise.
  • The molcajete/mortar method depends on patience and gradual oil addition.
