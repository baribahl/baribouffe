All-Purpose Roasted Vegetable Salsa

A roasted vegetable sauce for pasta, chips, sandwiches, or general spooning: tomatoes, peppers, jalapenos, onion, and garlic are roasted, then blended with lemon juice and olive oil.

Cookable Use caution

Ingredients

  • 6 Roma tomatoes, halved
  • 3 bell peppers, cut into large slices
  • 3 jalapenos, halved and deseeded
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 head garlic
  • Olive oil, enough to coat
  • Salt, to taste
  • Lemon juice, to taste

Effort

45 min

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Store refrigerated in a clean jar and use within 4-5 days.

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Put tomatoes, bell peppers, jalapenos, and onion on a parchment-lined baking dish.
  3. Toss with olive oil and salt.
  4. Cut the top off the garlic head to expose the cloves.
  5. Add the garlic to the baking dish and coat with olive oil and salt.
  6. Roast for 35-40 minutes, until the vegetables are softened.
  7. Transfer the roasted vegetables to a blender.
  8. Squeeze the soft garlic out of its skin and add it.
  9. Add salt and lemon juice to taste.
  10. Blend until smooth, drizzling in a little olive oil while blending for a glossy finish.
  11. Transfer to a clean jar and refrigerate.

Notes

  • The linked recipe lists refrigerated storage for 4-5 days.
  • The sauce is meant to work as pasta sauce, chip dip, sandwich sauce, or a general meal-prep component.
