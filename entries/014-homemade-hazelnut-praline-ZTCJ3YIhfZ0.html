Homemade Hazelnut Praline

A basic praline technique: roast hazelnuts, make a dry caramel, cool it, then blend the caramel and nuts until creamy.

Before You Cook

Use caution

  • Hot dry caramel burns quickly and can cause severe burns.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The video explains the method, but not the sugar-to-nut ratio. The entry therefore lists hazelnuts and sugar with unspecified quantities and flags that a tested ratio is needed before marking it reviewed. It also has a safety flag because dry caramel is burn-prone.

Instructions

  1. Lightly roast the hazelnuts in a 160°C oven until the inside is toasted but not burned.
  2. Make a dry caramel with white sugar in a saucepan. Stay close; dry caramel burns quickly.
  3. When the caramel is colored and starting to smoke lightly, stop cooking.
  4. Pour and spread the caramel out thinly so it cools quickly.
  5. Let the caramel cool completely.
  6. Break the caramel into pieces.
  7. Blend the roasted hazelnuts and caramel until the mixture becomes creamy.

Notes

  • The creator prefers white sugar because it is easier to judge caramel color.
  • Brown sugar is not forbidden, but would make color control harder.
