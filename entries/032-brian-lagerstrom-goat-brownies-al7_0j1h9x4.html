Brian Lagerstrom GOAT Brownies

A carefully developed brownie recipe using butter plus olive oil, white plus brown sugar, melted milk chocolate in the batter, Dutch cocoa, and chopped dark chocolate. The method emphasizes low heat, resting the batter, avoiding overmixing, and cooling fully before cutting.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

  • 100 g all-purpose flour
  • 1 g baking powder
  • 4 g salt
  • 30 g olive oil
  • 115 g unsalted butter
  • 150 g granulated sugar
  • 50 g dark brown sugar
  • 100 g milk chocolate chips
  • 50 g Dutch-processed cocoa powder, or 65 g regular cocoa powder
  • 5 g vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 85-100 g dark chocolate chips, chopped and sifted
  • Pan spray, for greasing

Effort

2-2.5 hr including cooling

3 / 5 difficulty

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 163°C without convection.
  2. Grease a 20 cm square metal brownie pan with pan spray.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  4. In a small saucepan over low heat, combine the olive oil, butter, granulated sugar, and dark brown sugar.
  5. Stir until the butter is fully melted and the sugars are incorporated. The mixture will look sandy.
  6. Remove from the heat and add the milk chocolate chips, stirring until melted.
  7. Transfer to a medium bowl and cool for 3-5 minutes, until warm but not hot.
  8. Whisk the Dutch cocoa into the warm chocolate mixture until smooth and lump-free.
  9. Stir in the vanilla and eggs, whisking thoroughly until fully combined.
  10. Rest the mixture for 10 minutes so the sugar dissolves further.
  11. Whisk again for 30-60 seconds until silky.
  12. Add the dry ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just combined.
  13. Fold in the chopped dark chocolate chips.
  14. Pour the batter into the prepared pan.
  15. Do not spread with a spatula; firmly tap the pan on the counter several times to level the batter without disrupting the brownie skin.
  16. Bake for 35 minutes.
  17. Check doneness with an instant-read thermometer at about 95°C, or with a cake tester that comes out with minimal sticky batter.
  18. Cool in the pan for at least 60-90 minutes before cutting.

Notes

  • The oil softens the crumb after cooling, while butter contributes flavor.
  • Brown sugar helps softness and flavor.
  • Milk chocolate rounds out bitterness; dark chocolate pieces give contrast.
  • The source says freshly chopped dark chocolate can be sifted so chocolate dust does not muddy the batter.
