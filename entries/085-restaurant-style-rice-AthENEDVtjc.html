Restaurant-Style Rice

Technique notes for better rice: choose fragrant long-grain rice, rinse well, use less water than the common overhydrated home ratio, cook gently, rest, and fluff.

Reference

Summary

Technique notes for better rice: choose fragrant long-grain rice, rinse well, use less water than the common overhydrated home ratio, cook gently, rest, and fluff.

Key Points

  • Use better-quality rice when possible.
  • Jasmine and basmati have more aroma than generic long-grain white rice.
  • Rinse rice in cold water 3-4 times to remove excess surface starch.
  • For long-grain rice, use 1 part rice to 1.25 parts water.
  • For stovetop rice, bring water to a boil with salt and a small knob of butter, then reduce heat fully and cook for about 15 minutes.
  • Rest cooked rice for at least 5 minutes before fluffing.
  • The creator says restaurants may use an oven or rice cooker for more even heat; that can take about 40-45 minutes.
  • The uploader notes that butter and salt are for composed rice dishes, not sticky rice.

Practical Use

  • Use this as a baseline for jasmine or basmati rice.
  • Rinsing matters most when the rice otherwise tastes gummy or tacky.
  • Resting is part of the cooking process; do not skip it.
  • Finish with sauce, herbs, or other additions after the rice is cooked.
