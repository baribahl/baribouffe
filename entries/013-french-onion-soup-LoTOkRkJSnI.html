French Onion Soup

A French onion soup built around deep onion caramelization, then simmered with stock and topped with salty croutons and Comte before gratinating.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

Per person:

  • 3 onions, thinly sliced
  • About 200 g water, vegetable stock, poultry stock, or meat jus

For the pot:

  • Balsamic vinegar, a little
  • Thyme
  • Garlic
  • Black pepper
  • Salt, carefully
  • Croutons
  • Comte cheese, generous amount

Effort

60-75 min

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: The torch finish is optional. A broiler or oven gratin is the safer default for most home kitchens.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The source gives the onion-to-liquid ratio but not exact amounts for vinegar, garlic, thyme, croutons, or cheese. These are intentionally listed as flexible quantities until the recipe is tested.

Instructions

  1. Peel and thinly slice the onions.
  2. Caramelize the onions thoroughly. The source says this is the most important step and can take 15-20 minutes or longer.
  3. Deglaze with vegetable stock, poultry stock, meat jus, or water. Use about 200 g liquid for every 3 onions.
  4. Add a little balsamic vinegar, thyme, garlic, and plenty of pepper.
  5. Simmer for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Taste and salt carefully. Remember that the croutons and Comte will add salt.
  7. Add croutons and a generous amount of Comte.
  8. Gratin with a torch, under a broiler, or in the oven until the cheese melts and browns.

Notes

  • The source chooses a vegetable-stock version for a vegetarian direction, but gives poultry stock and meat jus as alternatives.
  • The soup depends more on onion caramelization than on ingredient complexity.
