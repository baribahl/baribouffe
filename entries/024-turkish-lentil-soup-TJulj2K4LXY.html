Turkish Lentil Soup

A smooth Turkish-style red lentil soup with onion, garlic, carrot, potato, spices, and a buttery dried mint and Aleppo pepper finish. The flavor bomb can be stirred into the whole pot or spooned over individual bowls.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

For the soup:

  • 3 small carrots
  • 2 medium potatoes
  • 1 large onion
  • 3-4 garlic cloves
  • About 300 g red lentils, washed
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube
  • About 3 g ground turmeric
  • About 4 g ground cumin
  • About 1 g black pepper
  • About 10-12 g salt, plus more to taste
  • 1.9 L water, plus more if needed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • About 55 g butter
  • 15 g tomato paste, optional

For the topping:

  • About 55 g butter
  • About 1 g dried mint
  • About 2 g Aleppo pepper
  • 15 g red pepper paste, optional

To serve:

  • Lemon juice

Effort

60 min

Instructions

  1. Saute the onion, garlic, potatoes, and carrots in butter until they begin to brown and soften.
  2. Add tomato paste if using and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the washed red lentils, bouillon cube, turmeric, cumin, black pepper, salt, and bay leaves.
  4. Add the water and stir.
  5. Simmer until the potatoes and carrots are soft, about 40 minutes.
  6. Remove the bay leaves.
  7. Blend the soup with an immersion blender until completely smooth.
  8. If the soup is too thick, add more water and blend again.
  9. Make the topping by cooking butter, dried mint, Aleppo pepper, and optional red pepper paste in a small pan for about 2 minutes, until fragrant.
  10. Stir the topping into the pot, or drizzle it over individual bowls.
  11. Serve with more topping and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Notes

  • The pinned comment supplies the quantities; this entry converts the original volume and stick measures to metric.
  • The creator likes adding the topping directly into the soup for color and convenience.
