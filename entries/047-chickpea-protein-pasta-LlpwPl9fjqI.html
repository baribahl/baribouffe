Chickpea Protein Pasta

A vegetarian pasta using soaked chickpeas as a ground-meat-style protein base. The soaked chickpeas are blended with onion, garlic, greens, and salt, then fried thoroughly with vegetables, tomato paste, spices, cream, and pasta water.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

For the bean mix:

  • 100 g dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 handful greens, such as parsley, coriander, or dill
  • About 5 g salt
  • A splash of milk or water

For the sauce and pasta:

  • Oil, generous amount for frying
  • 1/2 bell pepper or another vegetable
  • 30 g tomato paste
  • About 5 g paprika
  • About 5 g smoked paprika
  • About 1 g dried oregano
  • About 2 g ground cumin
  • About 2 g ground coriander
  • 200 ml cream
  • 200 g pasta
  • About 100 ml pasta water, plus more as needed

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Do not treat the soaked bean paste as ready to eat. Cook it thoroughly; if using beans other than chickpeas, verify safe cooking times.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The source provides the main ratios, but the liquid for the bean paste and the frying oil are texture-based. The entry also remains safety- flagged because soaked chickpea paste must be cooked thoroughly before eating.

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas overnight.
  2. Drain the chickpeas.
  3. Blend the chickpeas, onion, garlic, greens, salt, and a splash of milk or water in a food processor until they form a paste.
  4. Let the paste sit for at least 10 minutes. It can also be prepared a day ahead.
  5. Fry the bell pepper or other vegetable until soft.
  6. Add the bean mixture and fry over medium-high heat with a generous amount of oil for at least 10 minutes.
  7. Start boiling pasta in a separate pot. Keep the pasta water.
  8. Push the bean mixture aside to make space in the pan.
  9. Add the tomato paste and fry for a few minutes.
  10. Add the paprika, smoked paprika, oregano, cumin, and coriander.
  11. Mix everything together.
  12. Pour in the cream and lower the heat to a bare simmer.
  13. Adjust seasoning.
  14. Add the pasta to the pan with about 100 ml pasta water.
  15. Mix well, adding more pasta water if the sauce looks too dry.

Notes

  • The uploader says chickpeas work best because other beans can take much longer to cook after processing.
  • The video shows kidney beans only because a few were left over from another dish; they are not the recommended base.
  • The pasta shape identified in comments is mafaldine.
