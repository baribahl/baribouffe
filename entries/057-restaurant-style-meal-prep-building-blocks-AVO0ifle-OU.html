Summary

A meal-prep approach based on restaurant mise en place: prep ingredients and building blocks separately instead of boxing the same finished meal for every day. The goal is fast assembly with more variety during the week.

Key Points

  • Use deli containers to store separate components.
  • Prep building blocks, not identical complete meals.
  • Examples in the video include roasted vegetables, jasmine rice, poached chicken breast for chicken salad, sliced onions, bell pepper, scallions, fresh herbs, pressure-cooked shredded chicken, miso-soy salmon cubes, salted tomatoes, bean salad, steamed potatoes, and a tzatziki-style yogurt sauce.
  • The method gives convenience while still allowing different combinations.
  • The creator estimates the work could be about 2.5 hours if done efficiently, though his own prep took about 3.5 hours split over 2 days.

Practical Use

  • Choose a few proteins, a grain or starch, vegetables, a salad/bean component, and one all-purpose sauce.
  • Store components separately so they can become wraps, bowls, salads, or quick plates.
  • Think about the ingredients you use most and find annoying to prep when hungry, then prep those first.
