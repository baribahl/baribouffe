Summary

A restaurant-technique reference video about making home cooking cleaner, faster, and more reliable by borrowing professional prep, storage, and flavor habits.

Key Points

  • Wash produce with a diluted vinegar bath, then rinse and dry before storing.
  • Use deli containers because they stack well and share lid sizes.
  • Make a parchment cartouche when a pot needs a temporary lid.
  • Soak garlic cloves briefly in warm water before peeling a large batch.
  • Bruise herbs before steeping them in soup or broth to release more aroma.
  • Keep mise en place visible and reachable.
  • Favor repeatable systems over improvising every small kitchen action.
  • Small storage and prep upgrades compound across a week of cooking.

Practical Use

  • Start with the storage advice: deli containers, labels, and consistent lids.
  • Use the garlic-peeling and herb-bruising tricks when batch-cooking.
  • Make a cartouche for shallow braises, sauces, or reductions where direct evaporation should be controlled.
  • Treat this entry as a pointer to a larger reference source, not as a complete indexed list of all 100 hacks.
