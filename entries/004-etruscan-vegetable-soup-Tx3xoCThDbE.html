Etruscan Vegetable Soup

A complex vegetable soup from Il Luogo Aimo e Nadia, presented as a descendant of Tuscan ribollita and Milanese minestrone. The dish layers separately cooked legumes, aromatic oil, vegetable broth, farro, staged vegetable cooking, and a cannellini bean cream so the final soup is full but not brothy. Instructions

  1. Soak the chickpeas, cannellini beans, and Sorana beans in abundant water for 24 hours in a cool, dry place. The source suggests about 8 times the legume weight in water.
  2. Add 1 garlic clove, 2 sage leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil to each bean pot.
  3. Cook the beans gently for about 1 hour 30 minutes. Cook the cannellini beans on the hob because they will be blended; cook the chickpeas and Sorana beans in the oven at about 120°C if possible, so they stay intact.
  4. The day before cooking, make the aromatic oil: crush 2 garlic cloves, sage, rosemary, pepper, and salt in a mortar until paste-like, then mix with extra-virgin olive oil.
  5. Wash and trim the vegetables. Put trimmings such as cabbage cores, cavolo nero stems, celery leaves, leek greens, roots, and potato peels into a pot of water to build the vegetable broth.
  6. Remove tough stems from the cabbages and spinach. Peel and cut the vegetables into small squares.
  7. In a pot, heat some infused olive oil and sauté the farro or chosen grain. Deglaze with about 4 ladles of vegetable broth and cook for about 20 minutes.
  8. When the beans are cooked, salt them lightly.
  9. Blend the cooked cannellini beans with some infused oil and extra olive oil into a smooth emulsion or cream.
  10. For the soup, heat infused olive oil with a pinch of fennel seeds and a bit more olive oil.
  11. Add celery, leek, and onion. Sauté for 5 minutes.
  12. Add carrot, mix, then add a little salt and broth. Cook for 5 minutes.
  13. Add chard stems if using, green beans, potatoes, Savoy cabbage, and kale or cavolo nero.
  14. Mix, add 1 ladle of broth, cover the pot, and cook for another 5 minutes.
  15. Add zucchini, spinach and/or chard leaves, 2 ladles of broth, and black pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  16. Add the cooked grain, chickpeas, Sorana or white beans, and cannellini cream. The final texture should be spooned and full, not watery.
  17. Serve warm; the chef suggests around 55°C for best flavor and comfort.

Notes

  • Background: the restaurant traces the name "Etruscan soup" to ancient ingredients such as cavolo nero and dried fava-like legumes found in archaeological analysis, then describes the modern dish as evolving from ribollita toward a softer minestrone.
  • The recipe is seasonal. The vegetable list can change as long as the staged cooking logic is preserved.
  • Farro can be replaced by another grain such as barley, millet, or rice.
