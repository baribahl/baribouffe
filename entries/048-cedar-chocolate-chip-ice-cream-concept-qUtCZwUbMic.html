Cedar Chocolate Chip Ice Cream Concept

A concept note for cedar-flavored chocolate chip ice cream. Western red cedar pieces are soaked, toasted, infused into an ice cream cream base, then paired with cedar-smoked chocolate elements.

Reference Use caution

Before You Use This

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Do not use unknown, treated, contaminated, or incorrectly identified wood in food. Cedar species vary, and this entry is not a safety- reviewed culinary formula.

Summary

A concept note for cedar-flavored chocolate chip ice cream. Western red cedar pieces are soaked, toasted, infused into an ice cream cream base, then paired with cedar-smoked chocolate elements.

Key Points

  • The creator starts with freshly chopped cedar pieces.
  • The wood pieces are soaked before toasting to encourage more even flavor extraction.
  • The soaked cedar is toasted at about 175°C for roughly 15 minutes, guided by smell and avoiding dark or burnt pieces.
  • The toasted cedar is added to a cream base, brought to a boil, then covered off the heat for 30-45 minutes.
  • The infused cream is strained and used as the base for a preferred ice cream recipe.
  • The base rests covered in the refrigerator overnight to develop flavor.
  • The chocolate component is designed to be more cedar-like by cold-smoking chocolate with cedar smoke.

Practical Use

  • Treat this as a creative process note, not a ready recipe.
  • Use a trusted ice cream base if testing a version.
  • Validate the wood species and food-safety status before any edible infusion or smoke use.
  • Keep smoke exposure controlled; bitter, resinous, or unsafe smoke can ruin the flavor and may not be safe.
