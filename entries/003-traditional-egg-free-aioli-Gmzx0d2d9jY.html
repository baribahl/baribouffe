Traditional Egg-Free Aioli

A traditional egg-free aioli made by crushing garlic with salt and slowly emulsifying it with olive oil in a mortar. The video emphasizes patience, thorough crushing, gradual oil addition, and steady mixing.

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: If storing leftovers, treat this as a fresh garlic-in-oil preparation: refrigerate and use promptly.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The pinned comment's garlic quantity is ambiguous. This entry uses 15 cloves because a viewer interpreted the spacing that way, but the amount needs confirmation before marking the recipe reviewed.

Instructions

  1. Put the garlic and salt in a mortar.
  2. Crush the garlic thoroughly until it begins to form a paste.
  3. Add the olive oil very gradually in a thin, steady stream.
  4. Keep mixing firmly and continuously while adding the oil.
  5. Continue until the sauce emulsifies into a dense, glossy, well-bound aioli.
  6. Serve fresh, for example spread on bread.

Notes

  • The source contrasts aioli with mayonnaise: this version contains no egg.
  • The video says crushing garlic releases compounds that help the olive oil emulsify naturally.
