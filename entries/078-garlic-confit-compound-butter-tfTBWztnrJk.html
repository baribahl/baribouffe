Garlic Confit Compound Butter

Homemade butter folded with garlic confit and salt. The cream is whipped until the butter separates, washed in ice water, then mixed with sieved garlic confit.

Cookable Use caution

Ingredients

  • 950 ml heavy whipping cream
  • 1 bulb garlic, cloves peeled
  • Neutral oil or olive oil, enough to cover the garlic
  • 9 g salt

Effort

1 hr plus cooling

3 / 5 difficulty

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Garlic in oil is not shelf-stable. Keep the garlic confit and the finished compound butter refrigerated or frozen, and do not store either at room temperature.

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 175 C.
  2. Put the peeled garlic cloves in a small ovenproof dish.
  3. Add enough oil to cover the garlic.
  4. Cover tightly with foil.
  5. Bake for 30-40 minutes, until the garlic is soft and confit.
  6. Let the garlic cool.
  7. Whip the cream on low speed until soft peaks form.
  8. Increase the speed and keep whipping until the butter and liquid separate.
  9. Squeeze as much liquid as possible out of the butter.
  10. Wash the butter in ice-cold water, changing the water until it runs clear.
  11. Press the cooled garlic confit through a sieve into the butter.
  12. Add salt.
  13. Mix until the garlic and salt are fully incorporated.

Notes

  • The leftover liquid can be saved for marinades or baking.
  • The quality of the cream affects the final butter.
  • Use this on eggs, savory crepes, biscuits, vegetables, steak, or bread.
