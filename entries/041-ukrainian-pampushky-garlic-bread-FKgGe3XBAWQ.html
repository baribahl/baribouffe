Ukrainian Pampushky Garlic Bread

Soft Ukrainian garlic bread rolls traditionally served with borscht. The rolls are made from a simple yeasted dough, then brushed after baking with garlic, parsley, salt, and oil.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 600 g high-protein flour
  • About 6 g sugar
  • 8 g instant yeast
  • 10 g salt
  • 340 g water

For the garlic oil:

  • 1 fresh or wet garlic bulb, or 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley
  • Salt
  • 60 ml neutral oil

For brushing before baking:

  • 1 egg, beaten, or vegan milk

Instructions

  1. Mix the flour, sugar, instant yeast, and salt.
  2. Add the water.
  3. Knead until a smooth dough forms.
  4. Put the dough in a bowl and cover with plastic film.
  5. For best flavor, refrigerate overnight. For a quick version, let rise for about 2 hours.
  6. If refrigerated, leave the dough at room temperature for 1 hour before shaping.
  7. Chop or crush the garlic and parsley.
  8. Mix the garlic and parsley with salt and neutral oil. Leave to infuse.
  9. Divide the dough into 8 even pieces.
  10. Roll each piece into a smooth ball, tucking the dough underneath.
  11. Place the balls in a large cast-iron pan or baking dish, leaving space between them.
  12. Let rise in a warm, dark area for about 1 hour, or until the dough no longer springs back quickly.
  13. Heat the oven to 200°C.
  14. Brush the rolls with egg wash or vegan milk.
  15. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until golden.
  16. Brush the hot rolls with the garlic oil.

Notes

  • The source describes fresh or wet garlic as fresher, crisper, and almost floral compared with dried garlic.
  • The bread is especially associated with borscht.
  • The uploader says high-protein flour is best, but other flour should still work.
