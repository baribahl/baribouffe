Soy Sauce Marinade for Ramen Eggs or Chashu

A foundational soy sauce marinade for ramen eggs or chashu, made with soy sauce, water, sake, mirin, garlic, ginger, scallions, and toasted star anise. Bring it just to a boil, then cool completely.

Cookable Use caution

Ingredients

  • 240 ml good-quality soy sauce
  • 240 ml water
  • 120 ml sake
  • 60 ml mirin
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 piece ginger, about 2.5 cm, crushed
  • 2 scallions, sliced
  • 2 star anise, toasted

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Keep the marinade refrigerated. If using it with eggs or meat, avoid cross-contamination and discard any portion that has touched raw or cooked protein unless it is handled as a used marinade.

Instructions

  1. Combine soy sauce, water, sake, and mirin.
  2. Crush the garlic and ginger.
  3. Toast the star anise.
  4. Add garlic, ginger, scallions, and star anise to the liquid.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  6. As soon as it reaches a boil, turn off the heat.
  7. Let cool completely.
  8. Store refrigerated and use for ramen eggs or chashu.

Notes

  • The pinned recipe says the marinade can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
  • A commenter asks about "reverse fermented water"; the pinned recipe clarifies this is plain water.
