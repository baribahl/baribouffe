Chinese Restaurant Beef Velveting

A Chinese-restaurant-style beef tenderizing technique: thinly slice beef against the grain, season and hydrate it, bind with cornstarch, then stir-fry quickly.

Reference

Summary

A Chinese-restaurant-style beef tenderizing technique: thinly slice beef against the grain, season and hydrate it, bind with cornstarch, then stir-fry quickly.

Key Points

  • Slice beef thinly against the grain.
  • Add baking soda for tenderizing.
  • Add water so the beef absorbs moisture.
  • Season with sugar, chicken powder, white pepper, MSG, sesame oil, neutral or olive oil, Shaoxing wine, and dark soy sauce.
  • Massage the seasonings into the beef.
  • Add cornstarch and massage again so the coating binds.
  • Stir-fry in a hot wok or pan.

Practical Use

  • Use this for inexpensive stir-fry cuts that need tenderness.
  • Keep the slices thin and even so they cook quickly.
  • Use only a small amount of baking soda when testing; too much can make the beef taste soapy.
  • After velveting, cook the beef hot and fast rather than stewing it.
Source notes

Source Notes

  • Needs a tested beef weight, baking soda quantity, liquid quantity, and cornstarch ratio before it can become a recipe.
  • Confirm whether Shaoxing wine should be replaced by rice wine when unavailable.
