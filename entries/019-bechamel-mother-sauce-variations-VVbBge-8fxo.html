Bechamel Mother Sauce Variations

A technique note for making bechamel and turning it into several classic or practical sauce variations: Mornay, soubise, mustard, Aurora, horseradish, and parsley sauce.

Reference

Summary

A technique note for making bechamel and turning it into several classic or practical sauce variations: Mornay, soubise, mustard, Aurora, horseradish, and parsley sauce.

Key Points

  • Start with equal weights butter and plain flour.
  • Melt the butter, add the flour, and cook the roux for 5-10 minutes until it looks like wet sand.
  • Heat the milk separately but do not boil it.
  • Add hot milk to the roux in small amounts over low heat, fully incorporating each addition before adding more.
  • Use a whisk once enough milk is incorporated.
  • Season the base with salt.
  • Optional traditional milk flavoring: onion studded with clove.

Practical Use

  • Mornay or cheese sauce: beat grated Gruyere, cheddar, or another cheese into warm bechamel over very low heat.
  • Soubise-style sauce: sweat onions until soft, then blend with bechamel.
  • Mustard sauce: add mustard, black pepper, and tarragon.
  • Aurora sauce: add tomato passata or puree at roughly one fifth of the bechamel volume.
  • Horseradish sauce: add grated horseradish, creamed horseradish, black pepper, and a touch of vinegar.
  • Parsley sauce: add lemon juice and parsley.
