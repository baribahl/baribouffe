Refresh Stale or Frozen Bread with Hot Water and a Hot Oven

A quick bread-refreshing technique: give stale or frozen bread a brief hot-water rinse, then bake it in a hot oven so the inside steams and the crust becomes crisp again.

Reference

Summary

A quick bread-refreshing technique: give stale or frozen bread a brief hot-water rinse, then bake it in a hot oven so the inside steams and the crust becomes crisp again.

Key Points

  • Use a quick rinse, not a soak.
  • The water adds moisture that steams the inside.
  • The hot oven dries and crisps the outside.
  • The source uses a 400°F oven, about 200°C.
  • Works best for sturdy breads such as baguette-style loaves.

Practical Use

  1. Run the bread quickly under hot water.
  2. Put it directly into a 200°C oven.
  3. Bake until the crust is crisp and the inside is soft again.
