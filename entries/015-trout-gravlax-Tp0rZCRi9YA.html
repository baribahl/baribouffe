Trout Gravlax

A trout gravlax inspired by salmon gravlax: trout fillets are covered with a salt, sugar, citrus, herb, and spice cure for 6 hours, then rinsed, deboned, skinned, and sliced thinly.

Instructions

  1. Prepare fresh trout fillets. Trim away the belly section if desired for another use.
  2. Mix sugar, salt, thyme, rosemary, chopped dill, lemon zest, pepper, sumac, and lemon juice into a homogeneous cure.
  3. Cover the trout with the gravlax cure.
  4. Refrigerate for 6 hours.
  5. Remove the trout from the cure and rinse it.
  6. Remove pin bones; the source says this is easiest after curing.
  7. Remove the skin.
  8. Slice into thin pieces, about 2 mm.

Notes

  • The source makes an argument for local trout over salmon, but the recipe logic is the gravlax-style cure.
  • This should be converted to a precise, safety-reviewed cure before practical use.
