Greek Roasted Chicken and Potatoes

A one-dish roast of bone-in chicken and potato wedges with lemon, yogurt, oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, olive oil, and water to create a pan sauce. The chicken roasts skin-side up until dark, crisp, and cooked through.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

  • 1.35 kg bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs and/or legs
  • 4 medium potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 30 ml olive oil
  • Juice of 1 large lemon
  • About 15 g salt, plus more to taste
  • About 1 g black pepper
  • About 5 g garlic powder
  • About 4 g onion powder
  • About 3 g dried oregano
  • About 80 g plain yogurt
  • 240 ml water
  • Whole unpeeled garlic cloves, optional

To serve:

  • White rice, pita bread, or Greek salad

Effort

90 min

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Put the potato wedges and chicken in a casserole dish.
  3. Add olive oil, lemon juice, yogurt, salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and dried oregano.
  4. Add whole garlic cloves if using.
  5. Mix very well.
  6. Arrange the potatoes underneath and the chicken skin-side up in one layer.
  7. Pour the water down the side of the dish so it does not wash seasoning off the chicken skin.
  8. Bake for about 1 hr 15 min, or until the chicken skin is dark brown and crispy and the chicken is cooked through.
  9. Serve with rice, pita, or Greek salad.

Notes

  • The uploader clarifies that paprika was mentioned by mistake; this Greek version does not include paprika.
  • The water creates a lemony pan sauce around the potatoes.
