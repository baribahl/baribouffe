Birria-Style Beef Tacos

Slow-cooked beef tacos built around a chile, tomato, onion, garlic, herb, and spice sauce. The beef is braised until shreddable, mixed back with its cooking juices, then folded into broth-dipped corn tortillas with Oaxaca cheese, coriander, and lime.

Needs details

Ingredients

For the beef and sauce:

  • 3 kg beef chuck or paleron
  • Salt
  • Neutral oil, for searing and frying
  • 8 guajillo chiles
  • 6 pasilla chiles
  • 2 arbol chiles, or fewer for less heat
  • 1 head garlic
  • 2 onions
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 1 sprig rosemary
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • About 15 g tomato paste
  • 3 cloves
  • About 3 g dried oregano
  • About 6 g whole cumin
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Beef broth, enough to cover the meat

For assembly:

  • Corn tortillas, quantity as needed
  • Oaxaca cheese, shredded, quantity as needed
  • Fresh coriander, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Extra cooking sauce, for dipping

Before You Cook

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The source provides the braising sauce for 3 kg beef, but not the number of tortillas, exact cheese quantity, coriander quantity, lime quantity, or final yield. Treat the assembly amounts as flexible until tested.

Instructions

  1. Season the beef with salt and sear it on all sides.
  2. In a separate pot, cook the tomatoes, tomato paste, onions, garlic, cloves, oregano, cumin, and cinnamon in oil for a few minutes.
  3. Add the chiles, bay leaves, rosemary, and thyme.
  4. Add water to cover the aromatics plus a little extra, then cook covered until the mixture softens and compotes.
  5. Blend the chile-tomato mixture, adding water gradually until it has the texture of a tomato sauce.
  6. Pour the blended sauce over the seared beef.
  7. Add enough beef broth to cover the meat.
  8. Cover and cook until the beef shreds easily, checking from about 3 hours. The source gives about 4 hr 30 min for the video piece of beef.
  9. Shred the cooked beef and mix it with some of the cooking sauce.
  10. Dip corn tortillas in the cooking sauce.
  11. Fry the dipped tortillas in a pan, starting with Oaxaca cheese so it melts.
  12. Add shredded beef and chopped coriander, fold the tacos, and cook until the tortillas are lightly crisp.
  13. Serve with extra cooking sauce and lime.

Notes

  • The cooking sauce is used twice: first as the braise, then as both tortilla coating and dipping sauce.
  • Oaxaca cheese is confirmed by the uploader in comments.
  • The arbol chile is the hot one in the mix; adjust for heat tolerance.
