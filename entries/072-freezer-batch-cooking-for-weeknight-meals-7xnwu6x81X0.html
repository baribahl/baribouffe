Freezer Batch Cooking for Weeknight Meals

A batch-cooking approach: cook many portions at once, vacuum-seal and freeze them, then use the freezer as a flexible weeknight dinner reserve instead of eating the same meal every day.

Reference Use caution

Before You Use This

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Cool cooked food quickly before sealing and freezing. Do not leave large hot portions sitting at room temperature for a long time before they go into cold storage.

Summary

A batch-cooking approach: cook many portions at once, vacuum-seal and freeze them, then use the freezer as a flexible weeknight dinner reserve instead of eating the same meal every day.

Key Points

  • The creator contrasts this with weekly meal prep: the goal is long-term freezer variety, not seven identical meals in the fridge.
  • Vacuum sealing is used to make frozen portions more convenient and durable.
  • The video claims about 30 portions from a 40 dollar cook.
  • Steamed broccoli appears as a vegetable component.
  • When steaming broccoli, avoid using too much water.

Practical Use

  • Cook a large batch when you have energy and portion it before freezing.
  • Freeze individual or family-sized portions depending on how the meals will be reheated.
  • Label portions with the dish name and date.
  • Keep multiple batch-cooked options in rotation so the freezer does not become another version of repetitive meal prep.
