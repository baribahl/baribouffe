Before You Use This

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Several tips touch safety rather than flavor: use sharp knives properly, avoid using an apron as an oven mitt, respect nonstick surfaces, handle hot grills carefully, and check appliances before leaving the kitchen.

Summary

A general cooking-skill checklist focused on avoiding common failures: poor searing, weak seasoning, bad timing, wrong tools, messy prep, and unsafe or careless kitchen habits.

Key Points

  • Pat food dry before searing so browning can happen.
  • Use acid to brighten sauces, vegetables, marinades, and cooked meat.
  • Do not start long-cooked aromatics with garlic if it will burn before the onion or other base ingredients soften.
  • Follow a recipe exactly the first time before improvising.
  • Avoid overcooking vegetables.
  • Keep knives sharp and use the right pan for the job.
  • Use enough fat and salt.
  • Clean as you go.
  • Toast nuts and spices for better flavor.
  • Pre-measure ingredients when timing matters.
  • Do not overhandle food that should stay tender.
  • Set timers before walking away from the stove or oven.
  • Wash rice and produce when appropriate.
  • Rest cooked proteins.
  • Preheat pans and ovens.
  • Taste as you cook.

Practical Use

  • Treat this as a pre-cook checklist when a recipe has failed before.
  • For searing, manage moisture first, then heat.
  • For flavor, check salt, acid, fat, and texture before adding more random ingredients.
  • For workflow, set up tools and ingredients before heat is involved.
  • For cleanup, keep a small reset habit: clear scraps, wipe surfaces, and wash used tools during natural pauses.
