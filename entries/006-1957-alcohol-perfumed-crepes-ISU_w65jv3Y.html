1957 Alcohol-Perfumed Crepes

A vintage Raymond Oliver-style crepe batter heavily perfumed with rum, pastis, beer, and vanilla. The batter is intentionally very liquid, producing very thin, light crepes with a strong aroma.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

  • 400 g flour
  • 8 eggs
  • 250 ml whole milk
  • 100 g butter
  • About 60 g sugar
  • 2 large pinches salt
  • 30 ml neutral oil
  • 250 ml amber rum
  • 50 ml pastis
  • 750 ml blonde beer
  • 1 vanilla pod

Effort

50-65 min

3 / 5 difficulty

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Source

Au Bord de l'Assiette

2026-02-07

Instructions

  1. Split the vanilla pod lengthwise.
  2. Heat the milk with the vanilla, salt, sugar, and butter until the butter melts.
  3. Put the flour in a large bowl.
  4. Add the neutral oil and eggs to the flour.
  5. Whisk, then add the hot milk mixture in 2 or 3 additions.
  6. Add the pastis and rum.
  7. Thin the batter with beer until it is much looser than a standard crepe batter.
  8. Cook very thin crepes in a hot pan. The source says the batter is fatty enough that the pan does not need extra butter.

Notes

  • The source says the recipe is "for 4 people" only as a joke, because the batch makes a lot of crepes.
  • The creator reports that the alcohol is still noticeable after cooking.
