Egyptian Macaroni Bechamel

An Egyptian-style pasta bake with tomato meat sauce on the bottom and a thick bechamel layer on top. It is similar in spirit to lasagna or pastitsio, with optional cheese even though the creator notes that cheese is not traditional.

Needs details

Ingredients

For the meat sauce and pasta:

  • 30 ml olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 450 g lean ground beef
  • About 4 g ground allspice
  • About 2 g black pepper
  • About 5-6 g salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 cinnamon stick
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube
  • 480 ml passata or crushed tomatoes
  • 240 ml water
  • Pasta, quantity not specified

For the bechamel:

  • 16 g flour
  • 28 g butter
  • 480 ml whole milk
  • About 4 g salt, plus more to taste
  • About 0.5 g black pepper
  • Small pinch grated nutmeg

Optional:

  • Cheese, quantity not specified

Before You Cook

Instructions

  1. Saute the onion and garlic in olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the ground beef and cook until no longer pink.
  3. Add allspice, black pepper, salt, cinnamon, bay leaves, and the bouillon cube.
  4. Add passata, then water.
  5. Simmer on low while cooking the pasta.
  6. Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente; keep it slightly firm because it will continue cooking in the oven.
  7. Add the drained pasta directly to the meat sauce and mix until coated.
  8. Taste and adjust salt.
  9. Make the bechamel: whisk flour and butter together and cook for 1 minute.
  10. Add the milk slowly, about a quarter at a time, letting the mixture come back up to heat after each addition.
  11. Add salt, black pepper, and nutmeg.
  12. Cook for a few minutes, stirring often, until thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  13. Put the pasta with meat sauce in a baking dish and spread evenly.
  14. Top with bechamel.
  15. Add cheese if using.
  16. Bake at 190°C for 30-40 minutes, or until golden on top.

Notes

  • The creator mixes the pasta with the sauce before layering, but says plain pasta on the bottom with meat sauce over it is another option.
  • Cheese is optional and not traditional, but the creator's family prefers it.
