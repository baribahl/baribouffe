Restaurant-Style Creamy Hummus

Technique notes for smoother, richer hummus: use dried chickpeas, soften their skins with baking soda, mellow garlic in lemon juice, whip tahini before adding chickpeas, and use more tahini than a basic home recipe might.

Reference

Summary

Technique notes for smoother, richer hummus: use dried chickpeas, soften their skins with baking soda, mellow garlic in lemon juice, whip tahini before adding chickpeas, and use more tahini than a basic home recipe might.

Key Points

  • Dried chickpeas are preferred for flavor, cost, and texture.
  • Soak dried chickpeas overnight so they cook faster and more evenly.
  • Baking soda helps break down the fibrous skins.
  • The baking soda step can also be applied to canned chickpeas, but they should be rinsed afterward.
  • Combine lemon juice and garlic first so the garlic mellows before blending.
  • Use a food processor rather than a blender for the texture shown.
  • Whip tahini with the lemon-garlic mixture before adding chickpeas.
  • Add water as needed to smooth the hummus.
  • Finish with harissa oil, cumin, and cayenne if desired.

Practical Use

  • Use this as a checklist when improving an existing hummus recipe.
  • If the hummus tastes sharp or harsh, let lemon juice sit with the garlic before blending.
  • If the hummus is dense, loosen it with water and increase tahini.
  • Cook chickpeas until very soft for a smoother final texture.
