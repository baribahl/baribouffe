Muhammara Roasted Red Pepper and Walnut Dip

A roasted red pepper, tomato, chili, garlic, walnut, and pomegranate molasses dip. The creator frames it as another Levantine mezze option that deserves more attention next to hummus.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

  • 5 red bell peppers, seeded
  • 1 white onion
  • 2 Roma tomatoes
  • 1 hot chili pepper, or more to taste
  • Olive oil, as needed
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 30 g tomato paste
  • 4 g cumin seeds
  • 4 g coriander seeds
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 15 ml pomegranate molasses, plus more for garnish
  • 100 g walnuts, chopped and toasted, plus more for garnish
  • 100 g pomegranate seeds
  • Fresh parsley
  • Pita or other bread, for serving

Effort

1 hr

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 200 C.
  2. Toss the seeded peppers, onion, tomatoes, and chili with olive oil, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, until the vegetables are slightly blackened.
  4. In a pan, caramelize the tomato paste in a little olive oil for about 5 minutes.
  5. Toast the cumin and coriander seeds for a couple of minutes, then grind them to a powder.
  6. Remove any blackened vegetables that are ready from the tray. The tomatoes may need longer because of their moisture.
  7. Add the garlic cloves to the tray and roast for another 15 minutes.
  8. Peel and discard the pepper skins.
  9. Add the roasted vegetables, roasted garlic, caramelized tomato paste, ground spices, and pomegranate molasses to a food processor.
  10. Process until smooth.
  11. Add the toasted walnuts and pulse until the dip reaches the texture you want.
  12. Garnish with pomegranate seeds, extra walnuts, parsley, and a final drizzle of pomegranate molasses.

Notes

  • The creator notes that breadcrumbs are traditional for body, but intentionally leaves them out for a denser flavor.
  • The parsley is presented as important for freshness, not just color.
  • Pomegranate molasses may need a Middle Eastern grocer or online source.
