Marinated Bean Salad with Mozzarella

A no-cook marinated bean salad for lunches: chickpeas, black beans, cucumbers, tomatoes, mozzarella pearls, red onion, olives, banana peppers, and herbs are tossed with a sharp-sweet dressing and left to marinate in the fridge.

Before You Cook

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The video is a meal-prep demonstration and does not provide full amounts for the dressing, tomatoes, mozzarella pack size, olives, banana peppers, or herbs. Treat these as flexible until calibrated.

Instructions

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas and black beans.
  2. Dice the cucumbers and red onion.
  3. Combine the beans, cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, mozzarella pearls, onion, Kalamata olives, banana peppers, and herbs.
  4. Whisk or froth together olive oil, red wine vinegar, banana pepper brine, Dijon mustard, and optional maple syrup.
  5. Pour the dressing over the salad and mix.
  6. Refrigerate in a sealed container so it marinates through the week.

Notes

  • The source prefers coriander, but uses parsley as an alternative for people who dislike coriander.
  • A commenter suggests keeping tender herbs separate if storing the salad for more than a couple of days.
  • Other comments suggest adding tomato only close to serving if excess water is a concern.
