Dense Caramel Ganache Hot Chocolate

A rich hot chocolate built from a caramel ganache: caramelized sugar stopped with hot cream, then emulsified with dark chocolate and loosened with milk.

Cookable Needs details

Ingredients

  • 80 g sugar
  • 100 g liquid cream, 30% fat
  • 200 g dark chocolate, 70%
  • 150-200 g milk, plus more hot milk if desired

Effort

15 min

3 / 5 difficulty

Before You Cook

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The source gives two milk cues: 150 g in the description and 200 g in the spoken method. This entry treats 150 g as the ganache base and 200 g as the dense hot-chocolate target until tested.

Instructions

  1. Warm the cream so it will incorporate into the caramel more smoothly.
  2. Make a dry caramel with the sugar.
  3. Stop the caramel before it burns by adding the hot cream.
  4. When the mixture boils again, turn off the heat.
  5. Add the dark chocolate and mix into a ganache.
  6. Add milk to turn the ganache into hot chocolate. Use less milk for a very dense drink, or add another 100-200 g hot milk for a lighter version.

Notes

  • The ganache base can also be used as a chocolate sauce.
  • The source intentionally makes a very rich, sweet hot chocolate.
