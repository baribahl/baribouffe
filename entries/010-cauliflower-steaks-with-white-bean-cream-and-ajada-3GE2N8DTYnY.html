Cauliflower Steaks with White Bean Cream and Ajada

Roasted cauliflower steaks served on a white bean and tahini cream, finished with ajada: a Spanish garlic, olive oil, and smoked paprika preparation used on fish or vegetables.

Cookable Untested

Ingredients

For 3 servings:

  • 1 cauliflower
  • About 2 g smoked paprika
  • About 1 g turmeric
  • 1 pinch thyme
  • About 1 g garlic powder
  • 60 ml olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

For the white bean cream:

  • 500 g canned white beans, drained
  • About 15 g tahini
  • About 2 g paprika
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt
  • Pepper

For the ajada:

  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 90 ml olive oil
  • About 2 g smoked paprika
  • 1 pinch salt
  • Pepper

Effort

65 min

3 / 5 difficulty

medium cleanup · 3 / 5

Instructions

  1. Wash the cauliflower.
  2. Cut 3 slices from the cauliflower, each about 3-4 cm thick. Work gently so the cauliflower does not crumble. Save leftover florets for another use.
  3. Mix smoked paprika, turmeric, thyme, garlic powder, olive oil, salt, and pepper into a marinade.
  4. Brush the cauliflower steaks with marinade on both sides.
  5. Place them on a rack, or on baking paper if needed.
  6. Roast for at least 45 minutes at 180°C. If using baking paper, turn them halfway through cooking.
  7. Blend drained white beans with paprika, lemon juice, tahini, salt, and pepper until creamy.
  8. Make the ajada: gently brown minced garlic in olive oil.
  9. Take the pan off the heat, then add smoked paprika so it does not burn. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  10. Plate a bed of white bean cream, add one cauliflower steak, and spoon ajada over the top.

Notes

  • The creator uses the ajada as the flavor anchor that makes the vegetable dish feel more complete.
  • Involving a hesitant eater in tasting the components is part of the video's practical advice.
