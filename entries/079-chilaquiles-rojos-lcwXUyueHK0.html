Chilaquiles Rojos

Fried corn tortilla chips simmered briefly in a charred tomato, jalapeno, chipotle, garlic, onion, and bouillon salsa roja, then topped with eggs, cheese, avocado, crema, cilantro, and extra sauce.

Needs details

Ingredients

For the chips:

  • Corn tortillas, quantity not specified
  • Frying oil
  • Salt, to taste

For the salsa roja:

  • 4 Roma tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 onion
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 jalapenos
  • 15 ml water
  • 4 canned chipotle peppers in adobo
  • Salt, source amount unclear
  • 1/2 packet chicken bouillon, packet size unclear

For serving:

  • Eggs, cooked to preference
  • Avocado
  • Crema
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Crumbled cotija cheese
  • Extra salsa

Before You Cook

Instructions

  1. Cut corn tortillas into sixths.
  2. Heat frying oil to 175 C.
  3. Fry the tortilla pieces until lightly brown and crunchy.
  4. Salt the chips to taste and set aside.
  5. Char the tomatoes, onion, garlic, and jalapenos under a broiler, with a torch, or over a stovetop flame until color forms.
  6. Peel the charred vegetables if desired.
  7. Blend the charred vegetables with water, chipotle peppers, salt, and chicken bouillon.
  8. Simmer the salsa.
  9. Add the chips and simmer for about 5 minutes, until they absorb salsa but are not completely mushy.
  10. Top with eggs, avocado, crema, cilantro, cotija, and extra sauce.

Notes

  • The creator prefers cutting each tortilla into six pieces rather than four for better chip shape.
  • Use enough salsa to coat all the chips generously.
  • Homemade chips stand up better to the salsa than very thin store-bought chips.
