Garlic Confit Hummus

Creamy hummus made from dried chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, ice water, garlic confit cloves, garlic oil, and salt.

Needs details Use caution

Ingredients

  • 400 g dried chickpeas
  • 5 g baking soda, for cooking
  • 60 g tahini
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 60 ml ice water, plus more as needed
  • 6 garlic confit cloves, or 2 raw garlic cloves
  • 45 ml garlic oil or olive oil
  • Salt, start with 6 g and adjust to taste

Effort

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: Garlic confit and garlic oil are not shelf-stable. Keep garlic confit, garlic oil, and the finished hummus refrigerated.

Needs details

/!\ DISCLAIMER: The pinned recipe gives volume measures for dried chickpeas and spoons of tahini, water, and oil. They have been converted to practical metric amounts, but chickpea and tahini weights should be verified before marking the entry reviewed.

Instructions

  1. Put the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover generously with water.
  2. Soak overnight.
  3. Drain the chickpeas.
  4. Boil for about 1 hour, until tender.
  5. Add baking soda during cooking to help soften the chickpeas.
  6. Skim foam from the surface as needed.
  7. Remove as many skins as practical for a creamier texture.
  8. Blend chickpeas with tahini, lemon juice, ice water, garlic confit or raw garlic, garlic oil, and salt.
  9. Adjust thickness with more ice water.
  10. Taste and adjust salt and lemon.

Notes

  • The creator emphasizes tasting the salt gradually rather than adding it all at once.
  • A commenter says the creator's garlic confit method is peeled garlic covered with oil, covered tightly, and baked around 190 C for about 40 minutes.
  • Raw garlic gives a sharper hummus than garlic confit.
