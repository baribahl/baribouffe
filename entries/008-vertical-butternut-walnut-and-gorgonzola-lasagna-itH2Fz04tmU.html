Vertical Butternut, Walnut, and Gorgonzola Lasagna

A vegetarian lasagna assembled as a spiral instead of flat layers, using fresh lasagna sheets, thin butternut slices, walnut pesto, spinach, thyme, gorgonzola, bechamel, and parmesan.

Cookable Use caution

Ingredients

  • 250 g fresh lasagna sheets
  • 1 butternut squash
  • 1 bag young spinach leaves
  • 300 g gorgonzola
  • 1 bunch fresh thyme
  • Olive oil, for the squash and dish
  • Grated parmesan, for topping

For the walnut pesto:

  • 200 g walnuts
  • 40 g bread crumb or soft bread interior
  • 120 g grated parmesan
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 120 ml olive oil
  • About 100 ml milk

For the bechamel:

  • 20 g butter
  • 20 g flour
  • 300 ml milk
  • Salt, pepper, and grated nutmeg

Effort

70-80 min

4 / 5 difficulty

high cleanup · 4 / 5

Before You Cook

Use caution

/!\ WARNING: The butternut is sliced very thinly with a mandoline. Use the guard or cut-resistant protection and stop before the squash becomes unstable.

Instructions

  1. Cut the butternut to keep the long neck section. Peel it, halve it lengthwise, and slice it into very thin 1 mm slices with a mandoline.
  2. Put the butternut slices in a dish with a little olive oil and massage gently.
  3. Make the walnut pesto: blend the walnuts, bread, parmesan, garlic, and olive oil roughly. Add milk gradually until the texture is paste-like.
  4. Cut the fresh lasagna sheets in half lengthwise so they match the width of the butternut slices.
  5. Spread walnut pesto on each lasagna strip.
  6. Add butternut slices, spinach leaves, fresh thyme, and small spoonfuls of gorgonzola.
  7. Roll the first strip onto itself, then continue wrapping with the remaining filled strips to form one large spiral.
  8. Place the spiral in an oiled round baking dish.
  9. Make the bechamel: cook equal weights butter and flour together for about 2 minutes over medium heat, then whisk in the milk gradually until smooth. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  10. Pour the bechamel over the lasagna, including the outside edge so the pasta does not dry out.
  11. Cover with grated parmesan.
  12. Bake for 35 minutes at 180°C.

Notes

  • The dish is visually impressive but mechanically fiddly because of the spiral assembly.
  • A round baking dish helps support the shape.
